Cold War

What Was the Cold War? (1947–1991)
- A long period of tension between the United States and the Soviet Union
- No direct fighting between the two → called a “cold” war
- Main conflict: democracy & capitalism (US) vs. communism (USSR)

Key Causes
- Disagreements after WWII about how Europe should be rebuilt
- Soviet Union wanted to spread communism
- US wanted to contain communism → “Containment Policy”
- Distrust grew between both sides

Major Events
1. Iron Curtain
- Term by Winston Churchill
- Imaginary line dividing communist Eastern Europe and democratic Western Europe

2. Berlin Airlift (1948–49)
- USSR blocks West Berlin
- US/Allies fly in food and supplies for almost a year
- Seen as an early win for the US

3. Korean War (1950–1953)
- North Korea (communist) vs. South Korea (supported by US)
- Ends in a stalemate → Korea remains split

4. Cuban Missile Crisis (1962)
- Closest the world came to nuclear war
- USSR placed missiles in Cuba
- US blockaded Cuba
- Ends peacefully after negotiations

5. Vietnam War (1955–1975)
- US tries to stop communist takeover in Vietnam
- War becomes unpopular at home
- Ends with US withdrawal and communist victory

6. Space Race
- Competition to dominate space technology
- USSR: Sputnik (1957) first satellite
- US: moon landing (1969)

Nuclear Arms Race
- Both sides build huge arsenals of nuclear weapons
- Fear of “Mutually Assured Destruction” (MAD)
- Led to decades of anxiety and drills in schools

Cold War Ends
- Soviet economy weakens in the 1980s
- Mikhail Gorbachev makes reforms: glasnost (openness) & perestroika (restructuring)
- Berlin Wall falls in 1989
- Soviet Union officially dissolves in 1991 → Cold War over
